Who regulates us

FINTRAC — Canada's financial intelligence unit

Established in 2000, the Financial Transactions and Reports Analysis Centre of Canada exists to detect and deter money laundering and terrorist financing. As a registered money services business, York operates under FINTRAC's rules with a full anti-money-laundering compliance regime.

Our registration FINTRAC-registered MSB · #M09351691
Our program Appointed compliance officer, client identification & record-keeping, ongoing staff training, and a regular independent review.

What to bring

Transactions over $3,000 CAD

For larger exchanges we're required to verify identity and source of funds. Bring these and you'll be in and out in minutes — no second trip.

STEP 01

Declaration of funds

A simple statement of where the money came from — employment, savings, sale of an asset, and so on. We'll help you complete it at the desk.

STEP 02

Government-issued photo ID

One valid, unexpired piece of photo identification that shows all of the following:

✓ Full legal name ✓ Photograph ✓ Full residential address ✓ Date of birth

Mandatory reporting

Some transactions are
reported to FINTRAC

By law, certain transactions are reported automatically. This protects everyone and it's standard at every registered exchange — there's nothing to do on your end.

LCTR · Large Cash Transaction Report Cash ≥ $10,000 Any single cash transaction at or above $10,000 CAD is reported automatically.
EFTR · Electronic Funds Transfer Report Wires ≥ $10,000 International electronic transfers at or above $10,000 CAD are reported automatically.
